General Nursing & Midwifery (GNM)

Program Overview

The GNM program provides a rigorous, skill-focused foundation for compassionate mid-level healthcare practitioners. The General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) diploma course is designed to train students in comprehensive patient care, preventive health, and midwifery. This program is highly practical, ensuring graduates possess essential clinical skills for hospitals, clinics, and community settings.

Curriculum Highlights

  • ✓ Medical-Surgical Nursing: Advanced training in critical care and surgical patient management.
  • ✓ Community Health Nursing: Focus on public health initiatives and rural healthcare delivery.
  • ✓ Midwifery & Pediatric Care: Specialized rotations in maternal and child health.
  • ✓ Psychiatric Nursing: Understanding mental health needs and therapeutic communication.

Key Program Details

Duration: 3 years (including 6 months internship)
Eligibility: 10+2 (Arts/Science/Commerce)
Annual Intake: Limited seats
