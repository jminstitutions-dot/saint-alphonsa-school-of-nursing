PROGRAM OFFERED

General Nursing & Midwifery (GNM)

The GNM program provides a rigorous, skill-focused foundation for compassionate mid-level healthcare practitioners.

GNM Program Overview

The General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) diploma course is designed to train students in comprehensive patient care, preventive health, and midwifery. This program is highly practical, ensuring graduates possess the essential clinical skills needed immediately upon entering the workforce in hospitals, clinics, and community settings.

Curriculum Highlights

  • Medical-Surgical Nursing: Advanced training in critical care and surgical patient management.
  • Community Health Nursing: Focus on public health initiatives and rural healthcare delivery.
  • Midwifery & Pediatric Care: Specialized clinical rotations focusing on maternal and child health.
  • Psychiatric Nursing: Understanding mental health needs and therapeutic communication.

Key Program Details

Duration

3 years (including 6 months of internship)

Eligibility

10+2 (Arts/Science/Commerce) from a recognized board.

Annual Intake

Limited seats available. Selection based on merit and interview.
